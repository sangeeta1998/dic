\begin{abstract}
The rapid proliferation of Internet of Things (IoT) applications has intensified the demand for efficient and adaptive resource management across distributed edge and fog infrastructures. However, existing approaches largely rely on static policies or prediction-driven optimization without accounting for the inherent uncertainty in resource estimation, leading to suboptimal performance and potential system instability. In this paper, we propose a risk-aware, AI-enabled resource management framework for SDN-based IoT-edge networks that jointly optimizes communication and computation resources under dynamic network conditions. The proposed framework integrates a machine learning-based prediction model to estimate future resource utilization along with an uncertainty-aware decision mechanism that guides resource allocation and task offloading. Leveraging the global network view provided by Software-Defined Networking, the system dynamically adapts routing and resource orchestration policies in response to predicted network states and associated risks. Unlike conventional approaches, our framework explicitly incorporates prediction confidence into decision-making, enabling robust and efficient operation in uncertain environments.

Simulation results demonstrate that the proposed approach significantly reduces energy consumption and latency while improving resource utilization compared to traditional static and CTMC-based methods.
